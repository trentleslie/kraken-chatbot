# BioMapper: Frailty WGCNA Discovery Analyses: Master Index

This is the entry point for the complete Kraken discovery-pipeline analysis of the Frailty multi-omics WGCNA dataset. It links every document in this body of work: the dataset composition, the two cross-module syntheses, the blind model evaluation, and the individual discovery and performance reports for all eighteen modules, each run on two models. Start here, then follow the tables below into any layer of detail.

## Executive summary

The work analyses 1,197 frailty multi-omics analytes partitioned by weighted gene co-expression network analysis into eighteen modules, running every module through the twelve-node Kraken discovery pipeline twice, once on Claude Sonnet 4 and once on Claude Opus 4.8, against the dev knowledge graph with BioMapper entity resolution. The result is a matched set of 36 discovery reports and 36 performance reports, two cross-module syntheses, and a head-to-head model evaluation.

The biology converges on a small number of cross-cutting surfaces that reproduced independently in both the Sonnet and Opus report sets, which establishes them as properties of the data rather than of the model. First, frailty inflammation is predominantly non-canonical: interleukin-6 and tumour necrosis factor alpha are informatively absent from most protein modules, which instead run through alternative receptor, alarmin, and chemokine axes, and only the Brown module carries the classical inflammaging hub. Second, the lipid programme is resolved into discrete biochemical stages across separate modules, and ceramides are reproducibly absent despite abundant precursors, placing the cohort upstream of lipotoxic sphingolipid accumulation. Third, gut-microbiome-to-host metabolism is the most pervasive cross-module axis and converges on colorectal cancer, the single most broadly shared disease association across the metabolite modules. A tryptophan fork (host kynurenine versus gut-microbial indole), net glutathione catabolism, and VEGF-independent vascular remodeling recur as secondary themes. The highest-value follow-ups are genetic and longitudinal: cis-mQTL mapping of PNPLA3 I148M, SULT2A1 and STS, GLYAT, and SLCO1B1 against the relevant module eigengenes, and longitudinal tracking of the Green and Black eigengenes to test whether the pre-lipotoxic state precedes the ceramide transition.

On the model comparison, both Sonnet 4 and Opus 4.8 produced high-quality reports, and a blind, randomized, pairwise LLM-as-judge evaluation across all eighteen module pairs (a ten-parameter rubric drawn from the hypothesis-generation and biomedical-faithfulness literatures, judged by Sonnet so that any Opus advantage is conservative) found Opus modestly but consistently stronger: it won eleven of eighteen modules and scored greater than or equal to Sonnet on every parameter, with the clearest gains in testability, novelty, mechanistic specificity, and groundedness, at roughly 1.2 to 1.5 times the cost. Hypothesis count did not track quality (Opus generated nearly twice as many hypotheses as Sonnet for Brown, yet the blind judge preferred Sonnet's Brown report), which is why the comparison is rubric-based rather than count-based.

The analyses ran on a pipeline hardened during this work. A triage concurrency fix eliminated a thundering-herd failure that had silently mis-classified well-characterized hub genes as cold-start at module scale (measurement failures are now zero across every module). A knowledge-graph reader-pool exhaustion incident, caused by an unbounded direct-knowledge-graph query fan-out compounded by back-to-back runs, was diagnosed against the Kestrel source, fixed on the client side with a concurrency bound, and cleared by a Kestrel restart. All findings are knowledge-graph-derived hypotheses with explicit evidence tiers, not experimental results, and carry honest caveats: entity-resolution errors recur in metabolite-heavy modules, disease associations anchored by high-degree hubs carry hub bias, the Grey module is the WGCNA unassigned bucket, and one Opus run (Tan) was degraded by a pathway-enrichment parsing defect.
