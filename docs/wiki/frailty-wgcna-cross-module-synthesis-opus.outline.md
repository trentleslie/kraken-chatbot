# Frailty Multi-Omics WGCNA Modules: Cross-Module Discovery Synthesis (Opus 4.8)

This document synthesizes the Kraken discovery-pipeline analyses of all eighteen WGCNA co-expression modules from a frailty multi-omics study, where every module was run on the **Claude Opus 4.8** model. It is the Opus counterpart to the [Cross-Module Discovery Synthesis](https://phwiki.phenoma.ai/doc/frailty-multi-omics-wgcna-modules-cross-module-discovery-synthesis-dGektQw73Q), which synthesized the Sonnet 4 module runs; the two are built by the identical method (per-module distillation followed by cross-module synthesis) so that any difference reflects the underlying reports. We read each Opus module report and distil here the dominant biological themes and the highest-impact novel hypotheses both within and across modules. Each finding carries the source report's evidence tier: `[KG Evidence]` for curated or database-derived knowledge-graph support, `[Inferred]` for pipeline reasoning over knowledge-graph structure, and `[Model Knowledge]` for language-model background knowledge. The per-module Opus discovery outputs are linked in the module compendium below and indexed in the [BioMapper: Collection Index](https://phwiki.phenoma.ai/doc/biomapper-collection-index-UcTfYSTchm).

## Overview

The three results that dominated the Sonnet synthesis reproduce cleanly in the Opus runs, which is itself the most important finding: the cross-cutting structure of the frailty network is robust to the model that wrote the reports. First, frailty inflammation is predominantly non-canonical, with interleukin-6 and tumour necrosis factor alpha informatively absent from most protein modules and only the Brown module carrying the classical inflammaging hub. Second, the lipid programme is resolved into discrete biochemical stages across separate modules, and ceramides are reproducibly absent, placing the cohort upstream of lipotoxic sphingolipid accumulation. Third, gut-microbiome-to-host metabolism is the most pervasive cross-module axis and converges on colorectal cancer as the most broadly shared disease association across the metabolite modules. Where the Opus reports differ from Sonnet is in emphasis rather than conclusion: the Opus runs make systematic analytical use of **informative absences** (a present receptor without its ligand, a present precursor without its product), lean harder into **therapeutic and pharmacogenomic** consequences, and more often propose **dual-function enzyme bridges**. Those differences are developed in the comparison section.

## Cross-module biological themes

### Frailty inflammation is predominantly non-canonical

The Opus protein modules converge, independently, on inflammatory programmes that bypass the interleukin-6/tumour necrosis factor axis, and they frame the evidence as a deliberate architecture rather than a coincidental absence. The Blue module presents a "receiver-cell" program in which tumour necrosis factor receptor superfamily members, checkpoint ligands, and endothelial markers are present while their ligands are systematically absent (TEK without angiopoietins, PD-L1 and PD-L2 without PD-1, TACI without APRIL), with interleukin-6 and C-reactive protein absent. [KG Evidence; Inferred] The Turquoise module is chemokine and interleukin-17 driven with tumour necrosis factor, interleukin-6, and interferon gamma all absent. [KG Evidence] The Magenta module routes inflammation through lymphotoxin alpha and the interleukin-33/ST2 receptor (IL1RL1). The Lightyellow module defines a thymic-stromal-lymphopoietin and interleukin-5 innate alarmin endotype with interleukin-4, interleukin-13, and interleukin-1 beta absent, distinguishing alarmin-driven from inflammasome-driven interleukin-1 signalling. [KG Evidence; Inferred] The Grey set recovers a multilineage cytokine state lacking interleukin-10 and interleukin-12 and proposes it is interleukin-27 driven, consistent with interferon gamma present but its canonical CXCL9/10/11 targets absent. [Inferred] Only Brown reproduces the classical interleukin-6/interleukin-18/CXCL10 hub. We therefore hypothesize, as in the Sonnet synthesis, that inflammaging here is a federation of receptor-, alarmin-, and chemokine-mediated programmes; the Opus reports add that the producing and responding cellular compartments are separable from co-expression structure alone. [Inferred]

### The lipid programme is staged, and the cohort sits upstream of ceramide lipotoxicity

The lipid-bearing modules each capture a distinct stage of lipid handling and together reconstruct an ordered cascade: free fatty acid overflow with incomplete beta-oxidation (Black), a long-chain acylcarnitine bottleneck at the carnitine shuttle (Royalblue), glycerophospholipid Lands-cycle remodeling (Pink, Purple, Lightgreen), a sphingomyelin reservoir (Green), and omega-3 membrane enrichment (Lightcyan). [KG Evidence] The Opus runs sharpen two points. The Black report frames the absence of ceramides despite abundant palmitate and stearate not merely as a pre-lipotoxic state but as potentially **actively suppressed** ceramide synthesis, a protective configuration against lipotoxic insulin resistance. [Inferred] The Royalblue report localizes the acylcarnitine block precisely: the exclusive C11-and-above chain-length profile with short and medium chains absent implicates allosteric carnitine-palmitoyltransferase-1 inhibition by malonyl-CoA rather than generalized mitochondrial dysfunction. [Inferred] As in the Sonnet synthesis, the reproducible ceramide absence across Black, Green, and Pink supports the hypothesis that the cohort is captured upstream of the ceramide transition and that module eigengene trajectory may predict it. [Inferred]

### PNPLA3 and the genetics of the lipid modules

The patatin-like phospholipase PNPLA3 recurs across the lipid modules (Brown, Pink, Green, Purple shared lipase neighbourhoods), and the Opus reports broaden the genetic framing from a single anchor to a set of testable pharmacogenomic hypotheses: PNPLA3 I148M as a modifier of the phospholipid modules, SULT2A1 and STS cis-mQTLs governing the Tan steroid-sulfate module, GLYAT genotype governing the Grey60 and Lightgreen glycine-conjugation signatures, and SLCO1B1 linking the Royalblue bilirubin and carnitine arms with statin-myopathy relevance. [Inferred] The recurring proposal is that single genetic variants modulate whole co-expression modules, each testable by mapping the genotype against the module eigengene.

### Gut-microbiome-to-host metabolism is the dominant cross-module axis

More modules are organized by gut-microbial metabolite production and hepatic phase-II conjugation than by any other process: intestinal barrier function and microbial indole tryptophan catabolism (Midnightblue), microbial polyphenol fermentation with secondary bile acids (Grey60), the FXR/FGF19 enterohepatic bile acid axis (Darkred), a specific Firmicutes fermentation guild (Lightgreen), microbial fatty acid co-metabolism with odd-chain markers (Black), and coffee-derived microbial sulfation (Darkgreen). [KG Evidence] The Opus runs add two mechanistic threads. The Midnightblue report argues the uremic toxins (indoxyl sulfate, p-cresol sulfate) reflect increased gut-microbial production rather than impaired renal clearance, because cystatin C and citrulline are absent despite the kidney-disorder associations. [KG Evidence; Inferred] The Grey60 report proposes an aryl-hydrocarbon-receptor feed-forward loop in which ursodeoxycholate and microbial phenolics co-regulate hepatic conjugation enzymes, a convergence node not raised in the Sonnet synthesis. [KG Evidence] A sulfation-predominant phase-II signature recurs across Grey60, Grey, Darkgreen, and Midnightblue.

### Tryptophan catabolism forks between host and microbial routes

Tryptophan handling again separates by module. Host indoleamine-2,3-dioxygenase kynurenine catabolism drives an immunosuppressive aryl-hydrocarbon-receptor and checkpoint loop in Blue and is shunted toward the neuroprotective kynurenic-acid branch in Magenta (kynurenate, xanthurenate, and N-acetylkynurenine present, the kynurenine intermediate absent), a configuration the Opus Magenta report links to the module's schizophrenia recurrence. [Inferred] Gut-microbial indole catabolism organizes Midnightblue with the host kynurenine pathway absent. [KG Evidence; Inferred] The fork distinguishes immune-driven from microbiome-driven tryptophan degradation by module membership.

### Oxidative stress recurs as net glutathione catabolism

Several modules read out oxidative stress as net glutathione breakdown rather than steady-state pools: gamma-glutamyl dipeptides with heme oxygenase 1 in Brown, a coordinated synthesis-utilization-recycling program with SOD2 and GLO1 in Turquoise, glutathione-depletion products (5-oxoproline, cysteinylglycine, cysteine-glutathione disulfide) in Lightyellow, and a gamma-glutamyl cluster with glutathione absent in Lightgreen. [KG Evidence; Inferred] The Opus reports repeatedly nominate gamma-glutamyl-transferase activity as the testable mechanistic anchor and predict that the glutathione-to-disulfide ratio should track these module eigengenes inversely.

### Vascular remodeling is recurrently VEGF-A-independent

Blue and Turquoise both encode vascular programmes with VEGF-A absent, and the Opus Turquoise report converts this into an explicit therapeutic prediction: an angiopoietin/PDGF-driven, Tie2-mediated vessel-stabilization phenotype on which anti-VEGF therapy would be ineffective and Tie2 agonists or PDGFR inhibitors would be the relevant intervention. [KG Evidence; Inferred]

## Highest-impact novel hypotheses

The following cross-cutting hypotheses are ranked by potential impact and breadth of supporting modules.

1. **Non-canonical inflammaging with separable cellular compartments.** Frailty inflammation runs through alternative receptor, alarmin, and chemokine axes; the systematic receptor-without-ligand pattern (Blue) implies the modules capture the responding compartment, informative for where to intervene. [Inferred over KG-evidence absences]
2. **An actively-defended pre-lipotoxic state.** Ceramides are reproducibly absent despite precursor abundance, possibly through active suppression; module eigengene trajectory becomes a candidate early-warning marker for the lipotoxic transition. [Inferred]
3. **Single variants modulate whole lipid and conjugation modules.** PNPLA3 I148M, SULT2A1/STS, GLYAT, and SLCO1B1 are each proposed as module-level genetic modifiers testable by eigengene cis-mQTL mapping. [Inferred; Model Knowledge]
4. **A microbiome-to-conjugation-to-colorectal-cancer surface.** Coupled microbial catabolite production and hepatic sulfation converge on colorectal cancer across at least eleven modules; Midnightblue's gut-versus-renal distinction and Grey60's aryl-hydrocarbon-receptor loop give it mechanism. [KG Evidence; Inferred]
5. **Carnitine-palmitoyltransferase-1 as the regulated lipid node.** The C11 acylcarnitine chain-length boundary (Royalblue) localizes the perturbation to malonyl-CoA-controlled mitochondrial import rather than downstream beta-oxidation, predicting a long-chain to short-chain ratio that tracks insulin resistance. [Inferred]
6. **Dual-function enzyme bridges couple metabolism to signalling.** Biliverdin reductase acting as an insulin/PI3K-axis kinase (Royalblue) and galectin-9 interacting with the insulin receptor (Blue) each link a catabolic arm directly to insulin signalling. [KG Evidence; Inferred]

Several within-module hypotheses are individually high-impact:

- **Imidazole propionate as a dysbiosis-to-insulin-resistance amplifier** (Brown), co-expressed with the cytokine and glucocorticoid program. [Inferred]
- **NAT2 acetylator phenotype as a hidden stratifier** of the caffeine module via the AFMU-to-methylxanthine ratio, available without genotyping (Darkgreen). [Inferred]
- **Lactate to NLRP3 to interleukin-1 alpha** single-step coupling of glycolysis to alarmin processing (Lightyellow). [KG Evidence; Literature]
- **SERPINA12 (vaspin) acting through downstream effectors** (FABP4, GLUT4, DPP4) rather than a general adipokine program, with a dipeptidyl-peptidase-4 inhibitor link (Royalblue). [Inferred]
- **Spermidine bridging the vascular program to the depressive-disorder signal** via polyamine-dependent autophagy (Turquoise). [KG Evidence]
- **Adiponectin absence defining adipose polarity** (Brown): the module captures the pro-inflammatory arm of adipose dysfunction with the insulin-sensitizing counter-regulator segregated elsewhere. [KG Evidence]

## Cross-module disease convergence

Colorectal cancer is again the most pervasive disease association, ranking top or high in Black (19 members), Midnightblue (14), Darkgreen (7), Darkred (5, alongside hepatocellular carcinoma at 6), Royalblue (4), Lightyellow (5), Green (3), Lightgreen (3), and Grey60, and it is predominantly metabolite-driven and gut-linked rather than protein-inflammatory. Neuropsychiatric disease is the second convergence: depressive disorder is the broadest signal in both Brown (21 members) and the Grey set (22 members), and schizophrenia recurs across Brown, Turquoise (via the spermidine bridge), Magenta (via the kynurenic-acid shunt), and Green. A cardiometabolic cluster (coronary artery disorder, essential hypertension, type 2 diabetes, obesity) recurs across Brown, Blue, Turquoise, Magenta, Royalblue, and Purple, and a barrier-and-immune cluster (asthma, psoriasis, panniculitis) recurs across Brown, Blue, Turquoise, Grey, Lightcyan, and Tan. The Opus runs additionally surface a **smoking-exposure axis** (23 of 46 Grey members and several Lightyellow members annotate to a smoking pathway), flagged as a candidate confounder of the barrier-stress signatures.

## Comparison to the Sonnet synthesis

The two syntheses agree on every dominant axis (non-canonical inflammation, the staged pre-ceramide lipid cascade, the microbiome-to-conjugation-to-colorectal-cancer surface, the tryptophan fork, glutathione catabolism, VEGF-independent vasculature) and on the same disease convergences, which is strong evidence that these are properties of the data rather than of the model. The Opus reports differ in three consistent ways. They make **informative absences** a primary analytical device, reading present-receptor-without-ligand, present-precursor-without-product, and present-hub-without-canonical-target as positive mechanistic signals (the Blue receiver-cell architecture, the Grey interleukin-27 inference, the Royalblue chain-length boundary, the Darkred 7-beta-epimerization deficiency). They more often carry a hypothesis through to a **therapeutic or pharmacogenomic** consequence (anti-VEGF ineffective with Tie2 agonists indicated; dipeptidyl-peptidase-4 inhibition via vaspin; NAT2 and SULT2A1 stratification). And they propose more **dual-function enzyme bridges** linking metabolism to signalling. These align with the blind judge evaluation, where Opus scored highest on testability, novelty, and mechanistic specificity. The Sonnet synthesis remains slightly stronger on narrative compression and on a few specific entity-resolution corrections, consistent with the modules where the blind judge preferred Sonnet.

## Methodological caveats and confidence

The same limitations bound this synthesis as the Sonnet one, and we state them explicitly. The findings are knowledge-graph-derived hypotheses, not experimental results, and the evidence tiers must be honoured. Entity-resolution errors recur in the metabolite-heavy modules (Pink, Purple, Lightgreen, Lightcyan, Grey60), where individual species were occasionally resolved to incorrect compounds, so module-level patterns are robust while individual species annotations should be verified. Disease associations anchored by high-degree hub entities carry hub bias and were flagged as such in the source reports. The Grey module is the WGCNA unassigned bucket and its coherence should be read sceptically. One Opus run (Tan) was degraded by the pathway-enrichment shared-neighbour parsing defect, which stripped its cross-entity themes; its entity-level biology is intact but its cross-entity context is thin. As before, the cross-module hypotheses are strongest where multiple independent modules converge on the same informative absence.

## Conclusion

The Opus eighteen-module synthesis confirms, on an independent set of reports, that frailty in this cohort is organized around a small number of convergent surfaces: a federated non-canonical inflammatory programme, a staged lipid cascade that places the cohort upstream of ceramide lipotoxicity, a dominant gut-microbiome-to-conjugation axis converging on colorectal cancer, and a tryptophan and oxidative-stress economy split by module membership. The agreement with the Sonnet synthesis establishes these as data-level findings. The Opus runs additionally yield a richer layer of testable consequences, genetic, pharmacogenomic, and therapeutic, which is the practical value of the more capable model. The highest-value next steps are unchanged and now better specified: cis-mQTL mapping of PNPLA3 I148M, SULT2A1/STS, GLYAT, and SLCO1B1 against the relevant module eigengenes, and longitudinal tracking of the Green and Black eigengenes to test whether the actively-defended pre-lipotoxic state precedes the ceramide transition.
