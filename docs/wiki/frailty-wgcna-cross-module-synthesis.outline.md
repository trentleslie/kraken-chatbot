# Frailty Multi-Omics WGCNA Modules: Cross-Module Discovery Synthesis

This document synthesizes the Kraken discovery-pipeline analyses of all eighteen WGCNA co-expression modules from a frailty multi-omics study, run on the dev environment with BioMapper entity resolution on 2026-06-23 (the Brown module reflects the corrected 2026-06-22 run). The eighteen modules comprise 1,148 named analytes (proteins, metabolites, and chemistry species) partitioned by weighted gene co-expression network analysis. We read each module's discovery report and distil here the dominant biological themes and the highest-impact novel hypotheses both within and across modules. Each finding carries the source report's evidence tier: `[KG Evidence]` for curated or database-derived knowledge-graph support, `[Inferred]` for pipeline reasoning over knowledge-graph structure, and `[Model Knowledge]` for language-model background knowledge; these tiers are preserved verbatim because they bound how far each claim can be trusted. The per-module discovery outputs and performance reports are linked in the module compendium below and indexed in the [BioMapper: Collection Index](https://phwiki.phenoma.ai/doc/biomapper-collection-index-UcTfYSTchm).

## Overview

Three results dominate this synthesis. First, frailty-associated inflammation in this cohort is predominantly non-canonical: six independent protein modules converge on alternative inflammatory axes with interleukin-6 and tumour necrosis factor alpha informatively absent, and only the Brown module reproduces the classical interleukin-6/interleukin-18 inflammaging hub. Second, the network decomposition resolves systemic lipid metabolism into discrete, biochemically ordered stages spread across separate modules, and a recurrent absence of ceramides positions the cohort upstream of lipotoxic sphingolipid accumulation. Third, gut-microbiome-to-host metabolism is the single most pervasive cross-module axis, coupling microbial catabolite production to hepatic phase-II conjugation and converging on colorectal cancer as the most broadly shared disease association across the metabolite modules. The sections below develop these themes, rank the novel hypotheses, summarize disease convergence, and state the methodological limits that bound interpretation.

## Cross-module biological themes

### Frailty inflammation is predominantly non-canonical

The protein-bearing modules reveal inflammatory programmes that do not run through the canonical interleukin-6/tumour necrosis factor alpha cytokine storm, and the informative absences are consistent and reproducible. The Blue module presents seven tumour necrosis factor receptor superfamily members with the ligand tumour necrosis factor alpha absent, and interleukin-6 receptor present while interleukin-6 and C-reactive protein are absent, indicating receptor-side modulation and trans-signalling rather than a classical acute-phase response. [KG Evidence] The Turquoise module is chemokine-dominant with interleukin-6 and tumour necrosis factor absent, driving adaptive leukocyte trafficking through CD40/CD40LG costimulation. [KG Evidence; Inferred] The Magenta module routes inflammation through lymphotoxin alpha and the interleukin-33/ST2 axis (IL1RL1) rather than adipose-driven cytokines. [Inferred] The Lightyellow module defines a thymic stromal lymphopoietin/interleukin-1 alpha/interleukin-5 innate alarmin endotype without interleukin-4 or interleukin-13, a profile therapeutically distinct from Th2-targeted biologics. [Inferred] The Lightcyan module signals an interleukin-4-receptor-driven alternative-macrophage axis, and the Grey unassigned set recovers multilineage cytokines with interleukin-10 and interleukin-1 beta absent. Only the Brown module carries the classical pro-inflammatory hub (interleukin-6, interleukin-18, CXCL10, and a dense CC-chemokine cluster). [KG Evidence] We therefore hypothesize that inflammaging in this cohort is a federation of distinct receptor-, alarmin-, and chemokine-mediated programmes rather than one interleukin-6/tumour necrosis factor process, with direct consequences for anti-inflammatory target selection. [Inferred]

### The lipid programme is partitioned into ordered stages, and the cohort sits upstream of ceramide lipotoxicity

The lipid-bearing modules do not duplicate one another; each captures a distinct biochemical stage of lipid handling, and read together they reconstruct an ordered cascade. The Black module captures free fatty acid overflow with incomplete beta-oxidation (medium-chain acylcarnitines and 3-hydroxyacyl intermediates) under PPAR-alpha/gamma/delta transcriptional control. [KG Evidence] The Royalblue module captures the next bottleneck, selective accumulation of long-chain C16 to C26 acylcarnitines without short-chain terminal products, consistent with a carnitine-palmitoyltransferase-II or very-long-chain-acyl-CoA-dehydrogenase block at the mitochondrial carnitine shuttle. [KG Evidence; Inferred] The Pink, Purple, and Lightgreen modules capture glycerophospholipid Lands-cycle remodeling driven by PLA2 family enzymes, hepatic lipase, and PNPLA3. [KG Evidence] The Green module captures a sphingomyelin reservoir of more than thirty species, and the Lightcyan module captures omega-3 polyunsaturated membrane enrichment with omega-6 species specifically excluded. [KG Evidence] A striking and reproducible absence threads these modules together: the clinically validated free-ceramide risk species are absent from Green, ceramides are absent from Black despite abundant palmitate and stearate precursors, and sphingolipids are entirely absent from Pink. We hypothesize that the cohort is captured upstream of ceramide-mediated lipotoxicity, and that the eigengene trajectories of the Green and Black modules may predict the future transition into ceramide accumulation. [Inferred]

### PNPLA3 is a shared genetic anchor across lipid modules

The patatin-like phospholipase PNPLA3 recurs as a shared enzymatic neighbour across the Brown, Pink, Purple, and Lightgreen modules, and two independent reports nominate the I148M variant (rs738409), the strongest metabolic-dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease risk allele, as a candidate modifier of module eigengene and individual phospholipid species. [Inferred; Model Knowledge] We hypothesize that a single PNPLA3 genetic axis modulates several otherwise-separate lipid co-expression programmes, a directly testable proposition through cis-mQTL mapping of the I148M genotype against each module's eigengene. [Inferred]

### Gut-microbiome-to-host metabolism is the dominant cross-module axis

More modules are organized by gut-microbial metabolite production and its hepatic conjugation than by any other process. The Midnightblue module couples intestinal barrier function (FABP2) to microbial indole tryptophan catabolites and uremic toxins (p-cresol sulfate, indoxyl sulfate). [KG Evidence] The Grey60 module captures microbial phenylpropanoid and polyphenol catabolism alongside secondary bile acid enterohepatic cycling. [KG Evidence] The Darkred module captures the FXR/FGF19 enterohepatic bile acid axis with comprehensive glycine, taurine, and sulfate conjugation. [KG Evidence] The Lightgreen module attributes a specific Firmicutes fermentation guild signal (butyrate, caproate) sharpened by the absence of propionate, acetate, and trimethylamine-N-oxide. [KG Evidence; Inferred] The Darkgreen module captures dietary coffee xenobiotic metabolism, the Black module captures microbial fatty acid co-metabolism, and the Grey set captures phenolic xenobiotic sulfate conjugates. A phase-II conjugation signature, with sulfation predominating over glucuronidation, recurs independently across Grey60, Grey, Darkgreen, Midnightblue, and Tan, nominating the sulfotransferase-to-glucuronosyltransferase balance as a candidate cohort-stratifying variable. [KG Evidence; Inferred]

### Tryptophan catabolism forks between host and microbial routes

Tryptophan handling separates cleanly by module. Host indoleamine-2,3-dioxygenase kynurenine catabolism drives immunosuppression in the Blue module (kynurenine co-expressed with PD-L1, PD-L2, and LGALS9, reinforcing checkpoint-mediated T-cell exhaustion through aryl-hydrocarbon-receptor signalling) and insulin resistance in the Magenta module (xanthurenate accumulation). [KG Evidence; Inferred] Gut-microbial indole catabolism, by contrast, organizes the Midnightblue module without host kynurenine engagement. [Inferred] This fork distinguishes immune-driven from microbiome-driven tryptophan degradation by module membership alone, a useful separation for attributing a circulating kynurenine or indole signal to its tissue of origin.

### Oxidative stress recurs as net glutathione catabolism

Several modules converge on oxidative stress read out as net glutathione breakdown rather than steady-state pools. The Brown module co-expresses eight gamma-glutamyl dipeptides with heme oxygenase 1 and the advanced-glycation receptor. [Inferred] The Lightyellow module presents glutathione catabolism products (5-oxoproline, cysteinylglycine, cysteine-glutathione disulfide) with intact glutathione absent. [KG Evidence] The Lightgreen module pairs cystathionine with the vitamin-B6 cofactor pyridoxal and gamma-glutamyl peptides, and the Turquoise module couples SOD2/GLO1 defence to transsulfuration flux. [KG Evidence] The recurrent pattern, degradation products present and intact glutathione absent, indicates active consumption under oxidative load and suggests the glutathione-to-glutathione-disulfide ratio should correlate inversely with these module eigengenes. [Inferred]

### Vascular remodeling is recurrently VEGF-A-independent

Both the Blue and Turquoise modules encode angiogenic and vascular-stabilization programmes with VEGF-A explicitly absent: Blue through placental growth factor and TEK/angiopoietin signalling, and Turquoise through an ANGPT1/PDGFB/TGM2 vessel-stabilization axis distinct from canonical sprouting angiogenesis. [KG Evidence; Inferred] This shared informative absence suggests frailty-associated vascular remodeling proceeds through stabilization and barrier maintenance rather than VEGF-A-driven neovascularization.

## Highest-impact novel hypotheses

The following cross-cutting hypotheses are ranked by potential impact and breadth of supporting modules. Each is a candidate for targeted experimental or genetic-epidemiological follow-up.

1. **Non-canonical inflammaging.** Frailty inflammation operates through alternative receptor, alarmin, and chemokine axes rather than the interleukin-6/tumour necrosis factor cytokine storm; six protein modules show the alternative axes with the canonical cytokines informatively absent. [Inferred over KG Evidence absences]
2. **Pre-lipotoxic state.** The cohort sits upstream of ceramide lipotoxicity; sphingomyelin and free fatty acid reservoirs are present while ceramides are reproducibly absent, making module eigengene trajectory a candidate predictor of the lipotoxic transition. [Inferred]
3. **PNPLA3 as a unifying lipid-module modifier.** The I148M variant may coordinately modify several lipid modules, testable by cis-mQTL mapping against module eigengenes. [Inferred; Model Knowledge]
4. **Microbiome-to-conjugation-to-colorectal-cancer surface.** Coupled microbial catabolite production and hepatic sulfation define a metabolite risk surface that converges on colorectal cancer across at least eleven modules. [KG Evidence; Inferred]
5. **Imidazole propionate as a dysbiosis-to-insulin-resistance bridge.** In the Brown module, imidazole propionate co-expresses with interleukin-6, interleukin-18, CXCL10, and the glucocorticoid triad, and is proposed as a gut-derived activator of p38-gamma/mTORC1 linking dysbiosis to the inflammatory-metabolic programme. [Inferred]
6. **Xanthurenate-driven insulin resistance.** In the Magenta module, an interleukin-33/ST2 to indoleamine-2,3-dioxygenase to kynurenine-pathway cascade culminates in xanthurenate-mediated insulin resistance, coupling immune activation to cardiac stress. [Inferred]

Several within-module hypotheses are individually high-impact and mechanistically specific:

- **Lactate to NLRP3 to interleukin-1 alpha** (Lightyellow): inflammatory glycolytic lactate drives inflammasome assembly, coupling the module's metabolite and cytokine arms through a single biochemical step. [Literature]
- **LGALS9 to insulin-receptor bridge** (Blue): galectin-9 interacts with the insulin receptor in the knowledge graph, linking galectin immunomodulation directly to insulin signalling. [KG Evidence]
- **SERPINA12/vaspin adipokine axis** (Royalblue): vaspin coordinates hepatic bilirubin conjugation and fatty acid oxidation through tumour-necrosis-factor/FGF21/PPAR-alpha signalling, with a possible dipeptidyl-peptidase-4 inhibitory link to incretin biology. [KG Evidence; Model Knowledge]
- **9-Hydroxystearate antiproliferative signal** (Black): literature-confirmed antiproliferative activity against colorectal cancer cells, the module's top disease association. [KG Evidence; Literature]
- **REN to AGT to prostaglandin-E2 to interleukin-4 crosstalk** (Grey): a curated renin-angiotensin-immune causal chain linking hypertensive and depressive disorders through a shared hemodynamic-cytokine mechanism. [KG Evidence]
- **SULT2A1 enzymatic gap-fill** (Darkred): the sulfotransferase links a substrate and product bile acid both present in the module, an explicit enzymatic edge currently missing from the knowledge graph. [KG Evidence; Inferred]
- **AFMU to 1-methylxanthine NAT2 acetylator probe** (Darkgreen): a caffeine-metabolite ratio that reports N-acetyltransferase-2 acetylator phenotype without genotyping. [KG Evidence]

## Cross-module disease convergence

Colorectal cancer is the most pervasive disease association in the study, appearing as a top or high-ranking shared association in the Black (14 members), Magenta (15), Midnightblue (11), Darkgreen (7), Lightyellow (5), Darkred (5), Green (4), Royalblue (4), Grey60, Lightgreen, and Pink modules. The association is predominantly metabolite-driven and gut-linked rather than protein-inflammatory, which strengthens the interpretation of a microbiome-and-conjugation metabolic risk surface rather than a single inflammatory pathway. Neuropsychiatric disease recurs as the second convergence: schizophrenia ranks first in the Magenta module (15 members) and recurs in Blue, Tan, Lightyellow, and Green, while depressive disorder is the top association in both Brown (16 members) and the Grey set (16 members); the kynurenine and branched-chain-amino-acid neuroactive metabolites provide a plausible shared mechanism. [KG Evidence; Inferred] A cardiometabolic cluster (coronary artery disorder, essential hypertension, type 2 diabetes, obesity) recurs across Brown, Blue, Magenta, Royalblue, and Purple, and a barrier-and-immune cluster (asthma, psoriasis, panniculitis) recurs across Brown, Blue, Turquoise, Grey, and Lightcyan. The breadth of these associations across independent modules indicates that the frailty network captures a small number of convergent pathophysiological surfaces rather than a diffuse set of unrelated signals.

## Methodological caveats and confidence

Several limitations bound the interpretation above, and we state them explicitly. First, the findings are knowledge-graph-derived hypotheses, not experimental results; the evidence tiers must be honoured, and `[Inferred]` and `[Model Knowledge]` claims require independent validation. Second, entity-resolution errors recur in the metabolite-heavy modules: individual species in Green, Pink, Purple, Lightgreen, Grey60, and Lightcyan were occasionally resolved to incorrect compounds (for example a lysophosphatidylcholine to a cosmetic ingredient, indolepropionate to a sterol, caproate to a pharmaceutical steroid ester), so module-level patterns are robust while individual species annotations should be verified before use. Third, disease associations anchored by high-degree hub entities carry a hub-bias risk and were flagged as such in the source reports. Fourth, the Grey module is the WGCNA unassigned bucket; its apparent coherence may reflect generic immune-hub co-occurrence rather than true co-regulation and should be read sceptically. Fifth, a parsing defect in the pathway-enrichment node dropped a subset of shared-neighbour edges in the metabolite-heavy modules when the knowledge graph returned a null node degree; the defect is recorded visibly in each performance report and does not affect triage classification or disease recurrence, but it modestly understates shared-neighbour connectivity. The triage classification itself was reliable across every module, with zero edge-count measurement failures. The cross-module hypotheses are strongest where multiple independent modules converge on the same informative absence (the non-canonical inflammation and pre-lipotoxic signatures), because convergence across separately-clustered modules is harder to explain by single-module artefact.

## Conclusion

The eighteen-module sweep indicates that frailty in this cohort is organized around a small number of convergent surfaces rather than a diffuse multi-omic signal: a federated, non-canonical inflammatory programme; a lipid cascade resolved into ordered stages that collectively place the cohort upstream of ceramide lipotoxicity; a dominant gut-microbiome-to-conjugation metabolic axis converging on colorectal cancer; and a tryptophan and oxidative-stress economy split cleanly by module membership. The highest-value next steps are genetic and longitudinal: cis-mQTL mapping of PNPLA3 I148M and SULT2A1/STS against module eigengenes to test the shared-anchor hypotheses, and longitudinal tracking of the Green and Black eigengenes to test whether the pre-lipotoxic reservoir predicts the ceramide transition. The per-module discovery outputs and performance reports linked above provide the full evidence behind each claim.
